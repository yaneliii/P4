Mediums

Acrylic Paint

Acrylic paint is a versatile and widely used material; it is known for its opaque vibrant colors and easy blending. However, it has a short drying time which gives the artist less time to work with the paint. Fortunately, you can just add a retarding medium to your paint that allows it to remain wet for longer. This gives artists more time to use the paint and even improves blending. You even work with its quick-drying instead of against it by using it to create layers or textures - as it dries with a durable, non-cracking finish. Additionally, acrylic paint can be mixed with other materials like water for a watercolor-like effect. Acrylics also adhere well to several types of surfaces, including canvas, paper, wood, etc., making them popular among artists of all levels. Overall, it is a great medium for beginners as they can attempt different painting techniques, and change the thickness of it, and generally cheaper.
